EOG ASSIST: EYE MOVEMENT COMMUNICATION SYSTEM

Biomedical Instrumentation & IoT Integration | Version 1.0

1. Project Summary

Clinical Problem: Patients in bedridden states (e.g., with neuromuscular diseases) have reduced or zero mobility and severe speech limitations, making it impossible for them to communicate basic needs independently to their caregivers.

Value Proposition: The system takes advantage of eye movement—one of the few motor functions preserved in these patients—to measure electrooculography (EOG) signals. These voltage variations are translated into commands sent in real-time via IoT technology, providing a non-invasive, accessible, and low-cost communication channel that substantially increases the patient's quality of life and autonomy.

Last Update: April 13, 2026

2. Device / Sensor / IoT Equipment

Hardware Components

  • Electrodes: Ag/AgCl surface electrodes.
  • Amplification: AD620 High-precision instrumentation amplifier.
  • Filtering & Control: TL084 or LM741 Op-Amps.
  • Processing: Arduino microcontroller for digitalization.

Technical Specifications

  • Variable: Ocular electrical potential (Corneo-retinal dipole).
  • Measurement Range: 10 µV to 5 mV.
  • Clinical Resolution: 10 µV.
  • Sampling Rate: 250 Hz (Nyquist compliant for signals < 50 Hz).

Power Source

Requires a symmetric power source (± 12V) for analog stages and 5V/3.3V for IoT modules. Estimated global consumption is low (< 500 mW).

3. Clinical Data Management & Flow

The system utilizes a continuous streaming pipeline where the ocular dipole position is measured in real-time. Data flows through these key stages:

Differential Amplification (G ~ 1000)
Active Low-Pass Filtering (52 Hz)
Analog Threshold Comparison
Cloud Streaming (JSON via Wi-Fi)

Connectivity & Redundancy

Loss of Connectivity Strategy: Local buffer storage in the microcontroller to retain alerts, followed by delayed synchronization with the cloud once Wi-Fi is restored.

Auditory Records

Audit Logs: Cloud platform (Grafana) maintains logs with timestamps, User ID, and specific actions (e.g., "Thirst command activated") for traceability.

4. Regulations and Compliance

  • IEC 60601 (Electrical Safety): Critical for isolating the patient from electrical shock or leakage currents when placing electrodes on the head.
  • ISO 13485 (Quality Management): Guidelines for biomedical instrumentation design and manufacturing practices.
  • Data Protection (HIPAA-like): Secure Wi-Fi channels to protect home privacy.
Risk Classification: Class II (Non-invasive monitoring device).

Out of Scope: FDA/COFEPRIS approvals are not included in this proof-of-concept due to time and financial constraints.

5. IoT System Technical Architecture

  • Perception Layer: Ag/AgCl electrodes, AD620 amplifier, and ADC on Arduino.
  • Connectivity Layer: Wi-Fi module using MQTT/HTTP protocols.
  • Processing Layer: Edge Computing for noise filtering and thresholding; Cloud Computing for visualization and alert routing.
  • Application Layer: Grafana Dashboard for real-time voltage monitoring and caregiver alerts.

6. Use (or Non-use) of Artificial Intelligence

The decision was made to NOT use AI for this version of the prototype.

Justification

Rule-based logic is sufficient because the EOG signal has a direct linear relationship with the gaze angle (up to 30°). Static thresholds are highly efficient and predictable.

Avoided Risks

  • Eliminates "Black Box" effects.
  • Avoids algorithmic bias and over-fitting.
  • No massive training data dependency.

Advantages

  • Lower computational cost.
  • Instantaneous processing (Edge).
  • Easier to audit under medical standards.

7. Users, Context & Ethics

Primary User: Bedridden patients with neuromuscular diseases. Secondary User: Caregivers or healthcare professionals.

Informed Consent

A formal document will be presented to the patient or legal guardian explaining the non-invasive nature of biopotential collection, cloud data transmission, and log security.

Equity Consideration

Bioelectric EOG measures the retinal dipole. It does not rely on optical cameras, making it equitable across different skin tones, ethnicities, and facial physical characteristics.

8. Scope, Limitations & Future Work

In Scope

Functional analog prototype, Wi-Fi transmission, real-time Grafana visualization, and basic threshold-based alerts (Right/Left gaze).

Limitations

Susceptibility to facial muscle noise (EMG), electrode impedance changes over time, and environmental electrical interference.

Future Roadmap

  • Implementation of digital signal processing (DSP) for noise reduction.
  • Miniaturization into a wearable PCB.
  • Development of a mobile app for caregiver notifications.
