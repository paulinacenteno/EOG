EOG ASSIST: EYE MOVEMENT COMMUNICATION SYSTEM

1. Project Summary

Clinical problem: Patients in a bedridden state have reduced mobility and severe or no speech capabilities, making them unable to efficiently communicate their basic needs.

Value proposition: The system uses electrooculography (EOG) signals to translate eye movements into automated commands sent in real time via IoT, providing a non-invasive, accessible, and low-cost communication alternative.

2. Device / Sensor / IoT Equipment Description

Sensors and Devices

  • 1 AD620 instrumentation amplifier
  • TL084 operational amplifiers
  • Arduino
  • Surface electrodes (Ag/AgCl)

Physiological variable measured

  • Ocular electrical potential (EOG)
  • Unit: Volts (V-mV)

Measurement & Sampling

  • Measurement range: 10 µV to 5 mV
  • Minimum required resolution: 10 µV
  • Sampling frequency: 250 Hz. EOG signals are low frequency (<50 Hz), so this rate avoids aliasing and captures dynamic changes.

Communication Protocol

  • WiFi
  • Protocols: HTTP or MQTT for IoT cloud connectivity

Power supply

  • Analog stage: ±12 V
  • Transmission module: 3.3V to 5V
  • Estimated consumption: Low (<500 mW)

3. Clinical Data Management and Flow

Data pipeline

Continuous streaming: The EOG signal changes in real time according to eye movement relative to the electrodes.

On-device preprocessing:

  • Differential amplification (high CMRR)
  • Low-pass filtering
  • Comparators for direction detection

6. No Use of Artificial Intelligence

Why is rule-based or threshold logic sufficient?

EOG is directly linear with gaze angle for deviations up to approximately 30°. Using operational amplifiers configured as voltage comparators establishes efficient static thresholds for direction (e.g., positive = right, negative = left) without complex algorithmic processing.

Is there clinical evidence supporting these thresholds?

Yes, clinical guidelines from the American Academy of Sleep Medicine (AASM) support lateral electrode placement and document that eye movements generate waveforms with opposite phases or polarities.

Risks avoided by not using AI

Black box effect, algorithmic bias and dependency on prior data collection for training across different patients.

When would AI be appropriate in the future?

If there is a need to decode rapid patterns, classify complex noise (such as intentional blinks), or use smartphone cameras (computer vision) to eliminate facial electrodes.

Advantages

Greater transparency, lower computational cost, easier auditing. The circuit behavior is fully predictable and explainable, with reduced latency and simpler validation under medical hardware regulations.

7. Users, Clinical Context, and Ethical Considerations

Informed consent

Explaining in non-technical language the non-invasive nature of EOG biopotential collection, the purpose of transmitting commands to the cloud, the technical limitations of the prototype, and the commitment to secure data handling.

Clinical risk analysis

  • False positive (incorrect message): A blink or muscle spasm activates the threshold and sends an unintended command. Consequence: Caregiver fatigue and alarm overload.
  • False negative (ignored need): The patient performs the eye movement, but a dry electrode or baseline drift hides the signal. Consequence: Patient distress due to unmet physiological needs.

Equity considerations

EOG measures the retinal dipole, so it is independent of skin tone, ethnicity, or facial visual characteristics.

8. Scope, Limitations, and Future Work

In scope

Delivery of an analog prototype that captures horizontal and vertical eye biopotentials. Includes wireless transmission to an IoT server and real-time visualization in Grafana with basic alert triggering.

Out of scope

Medical certifications, commercial PCB design and manufacturing, and mobile app development. This is due to time constraints, limited financial resources, and clinical regulations requiring extensive testing and validation.

Hardware or data model limitations

Noise, user movement, electrode dependency.

Future Roadmap

  • Improve signal filtering.
  • Develop a mobile app capable of detecting eye movements using the phone camera without relying on electrodes.
